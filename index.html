Congrats — You're Teamwork Masters
You just reverse-engineered what makes a story a story.

Some elements — like conflict — show up in almost every story. But others — like a villain or a happy ending — don't always make the cut. That's the interesting part. Stories share some ingredients, but not all of them. So what's required, and what's a choice the author makes?

If stories follow patterns, then authors are making choices — about what to include, what to leave out, and whose perspective to center. Stories don't just tell reality. They construct it.
