Here's what you just figured out.

Most stories share the same building blocks: characters, conflict, a sequence of events, and change. Even when stories feel totally different on the surface, those ingredients usually show up.

If stories follow patterns, then authors are making choices — about what to include, what to leave out, and whose perspective to center. Stories don't just tell reality. They construct it.
One last thought.
If authors choose what goes into a story — what does that mean about the stories YOU consume every day?
